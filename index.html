Healing Waters & Forest Rituals

Shichirigahama Coastal Onsen

Private oceanside baths where healing mineral waters meet breathtaking Pacific views. Enjoy golden‑hour sunsets over Mount Fuji while soaking meditatively, and evening soaks under a canopy of stars invite deep relaxation and renewal.

Beppu’s Legendary “Hells”

Experience Japan’s most famous hot spring city with private access to the renowned thermal pools and geothermal wonders. Includes personalized mud and sand treatments.

Iwaso Ryokan’s Sacred Waters

Bathe in the centuries‑old healing waters of Miyajima Island while enjoying a traditional tea ceremony and restorative herbal infusions. A truly iconic ryokan experience.

Forest Bathing & Rituals

Guided silent walks through ancient groves, breathwork beneath maple canopies, nature journaling and sunrise meditation among bamboo—practices designed to calm the nervous system and honor ancestral connections to the natural world.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who is this retreat designed for?

Our Rest as Resistance journey is created for Black women seeking a deeply restorative experience combining Japanese wellness traditions and Black liberation practices. All identities are welcome to apply, but priority and pricing are curated for our primary audience.

What’s included in the price?

Your journey covers lodging, intercity transport, daily onsen rituals, workshops, curated meals and concierge support. International airfare, visas, travel insurance and add‑ons are extra.

How do I secure my spot?

Click “Pay Deposit” under your preferred journey to submit your deposit via Stripe. You may also submit an inquiry form below; our team will reach out with next steps and payment instructions.

What if I need to cancel?

We understand plans change. Deposits are refundable up to 90 days before departure, minus a small administrative fee. If you need to cancel after that, we’ll work with you on a case‑by‑case basis.

Meet Your Host

Dr Dédé Tetsubayashi is a healer, scholar and advocate who has called Japan home for over fifteen years. Her life’s work sits at the intersection of somatic healing, Black liberation and cultural exchange. Having walked sacred paths and soaked in hidden onsens herself, she brings an insider’s eye and a nurturer’s heart to every detail of this journey.

As your host, she curates deeply restorative experiences rooted in Japanese tradition and diasporic Black practices. She will introduce you to her favorite ryokans, demystify onsen etiquette and hold space for you to rest and reconnect. Join her to reclaim rest as a radical act of self‑love and resistance.
