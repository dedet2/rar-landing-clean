Healing Waters & Forest Rituals

Shichirigahama Coastal Onsen

Private oceanside baths where healing mineral waters meet breathtaking Pacific views. Evening soaks under a canopy of stars invite deep relaxation and renewal.

Beppu’s Legendary “Hells”

Experience Japan’s most famous hot spring city with private access to the renowned thermal pools and geothermal wonders. Includes personalised mud and sand treatments.

Iwaso Ryokan’s Sacred Waters

Bathe in the centuries‑old healing waters of Miyajima Island while enjoying a traditional tea ceremony and restorative herbal infusions. A truly iconic ryokan experience.

Forest Bathing & Rituals

Guided silent walks through ancient groves, breathwork beneath maple canopies, nature journaling and sunrise meditation among bamboo—practices designed to calm the nervous system and honour ancestral connections to the natural world.

Meet Dr. Dédé

Dr. Dédé Tetsubayashi is a healer, scholar and advocate who has called Japan home for over fifteen years. Her life's work sits at the intersection of somatic healing, Black liberation and cultural exchange. Having walked the sacred paths and soaked in the most secluded onsens herself, she brings an insider’s eye and a nurturer’s heart to every detail of this journey.

As your guide and host, Dr. Dédé curates deeply restorative experiences rooted in Japanese tradition and diasporic Black practices. She will introduce you to her favourite ryokans, demystify onsen etiquette and hold space for you to rest and reconnect. Join her to reclaim rest as a radical act of self‑love and resistance.

Senanu Kimono

Senanu is Dr. Dédé’s bespoke kimono brand where Japanese craft meets African aesthetics. Each piece blends silk and wax‑print fabrics, honouring the intertwined histories of two cultures while creating something entirely new. During the journey you’ll have the opportunity to wear an exclusive Senanu yukata or kimono designed just for you.

Guests in the VIP Sanctuary tier receive a custom Senanu yukata as part of their welcome gift. Travellers in other tiers can purchase a piece à la carte to commemorate their experience. Imagine a circle of Black women draped in vibrant textiles, sipping tea and walking temple paths—embodying both heritage and future.

Follow Senanu on Facebook and Instagram to see more Afro‑Japanese designs and behind‑the‑scenes moments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is this retreat for?

This journey is crafted for Black women seeking radical rest, healing and connection. Our group size of 6–8 guests ensures an intimate and supportive experience.

What’s included in the price?

Your investment covers lodging in boutique hotels and ryokans, all intercity transport, curated onsen and wellness experiences, two Rest as Resistance workshops, daily meals and white‑glove concierge service.

How do I reserve my spot?

Submit the enquiry form below or proceed directly to your secure deposit. Once your deposit is received, our concierge team will be in touch to finalise details and support your planning.

What if I need to cancel?

We understand that life happens. Deposits are refundable up to 90 days before departure. Please contact us as soon as possible to discuss any changes.
